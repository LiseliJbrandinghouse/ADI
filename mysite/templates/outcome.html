THEMATIC WORK GROUPS

Each of the TWGs will present a report to the main plenary for resolution and adoption. The TWGs will begin their work months prior to the Conference and develop abstract concepts for adoption by the main plenary in Lusaka. The conference will therefore work on the back to front model strategized around the following TWGs:

  1. The Constitution/Proclamation Act
  2. Democracy and Good Governance
  3. Structure and Systems of Governance
  4. The Diaspora Citizenship of Africa
  5. The Afriq as Africa’s Virtual Currency
  6. The Virtual State of Africa
  7. The Pan-African Fund for Political Parties
  8. Africa’s Sustainable Development Goals
  9. Commodities, Trade & Investments
  10. Immediate Measures to Protect African Lives

THE EXPECTED OUTCOME

As an historic epoch of our human endeavor and as Africa’s freedom writers of the 21st Century, the people will, at this conference: proclaim the STATE OF AFRICA by themselves and establish its interim government and governance structure; elect a leadership proviso by an open and transparent one-man-one-vote virtual system through which millions of the people of Africa aged 16 years and above will take part in the virtual vote that will establish the government of Africa. The conference will also reverse the result of the ill-intended 1884 Berlin Conference which set the stage for full African colonialism and thus the need for reparations, by creating a federation towards a federal state. It is, thus, set forth here upon us, that in our aspiration and in full recognition of our value for human freedom, dignity, and the exercise thereof, of our right to self-determination, that we the people take responsibilities for the construction of ONE NATION AND STATE OF AFRICA will, by all peaceful and democratic means, be established at this Conference.

Our action will include among many other things, the craft of the next steps to achieving all the goals thereby prescribed by the Conference on all other matters including the need for Africa to create an OVERSEAS CITIZEN CARD—similar to that of India as a way of strengthening Africa in skills transfer and investments, on one hand, and on the other, illuminate THE RIGHT TO RETURN TO THE MOTHERLAND FOR ALL AFRICANS, especially victims of colonial slavery. This includes the Caribbean countries’ right to take part in Africa’s matters. Another anticipated outcome at this conference will be the adoption of the Africa Sustainable Development Goals to attend to both our natural world and socio-economic endeavours.
